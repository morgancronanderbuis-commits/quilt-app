# Pattern: 2nd Easiest Jelly Roll Quilt Ever

**Approximate Finished Size**: 50" x 64"

---

## Materials

| Material | Amount |
|---|---|
| One jelly roll or 40 assorted 2½" x 42" strips | — |
| Backing | 5/8 yard |
| Binding | 3¼ yards |
| Batting | 60" x 74" |

---

## Instructions

1. Pair jelly roll strips into **twenty sets of two**.

2. Sew strips together **lengthwise**.

3. Press seams toward the **darker** of the two strips. Fold strip set in half lengthwise. Cut apart on fold. Turn one half of strip set **180°** so that opposite colors are next to each other.

4. Sew strip sets together again.

5. Repeat until all strip sets are assembled.

6. **Join blocks** together in rows following quilt layout. Make **20 blocks**. Trim blocks to **10" in width**.

7. Cut backing fabric into two pieces **42" x 58½"**. Sew pieces together in a **58½" seam**. Press seam open. (The seam will be crosswise on the back of the quilt.)

8. Cut **2½" bias strips** from binding fabric.

9. Layer backing, batting, and top. Quilt as desired.

10. Sew binding strips together end to end in **diagonal seams**. Press seams open. Fold binding in half wrong sides together. Place binding right sides together with quilt top. Sew in a **¼" seam**. Bring folded edge of binding to the back of the quilt and **slipstitch** in place.

**Enjoy!**

---

## Tips (Handwritten Notes)

- **Try to have seams press each other** — iron towards the dark.
- **⅜" rule**: if you can't see it from 3 feet away, it's meant to be there.
- **InStyle prints** — don't go out more than 3x; after 3x it's meant to be there.
- Use a ruler adjacent — 1 finger on obj roller + rest of fingers on ruler anchors it.
- **45mm rotary cutter** can do 8 layers.
- Fold seams towards dark fabric.
- **Stretch side to side** — lengthwise side to side is vertical/horizontal (not stretching).
- Don't use crosswise on the border — things will get wavy.
- Don't over-press — handwash or use a lingerie bag.
- **Crosswise grain (stretching) = horizontal.**
- **Chain piecing**: sew pieces one after another without cutting thread between — saves time and thread.
