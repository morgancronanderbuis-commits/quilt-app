# Pattern: Easiest Ever Jelly Roll Throw

**Approximate Finished Size**: 61" x 77" (Twin size)

---

## Materials

| Material | Amount |
|---|---|
| One jelly roll | — |
| Inner border | ¾ yard |
| Outer border & binding | 2 yards |
| Backing | 4 yards |
| Batting | Twin size |

---

## Instructions

1. Sew each 2½" strip **end to end with a diagonal seam**. This will make one continuous strip approximately 1,600" long. (Jelly Rolls contain 40 strips — no need to worry about fabric placement; there's no way to judge which fabrics will end up next to one another.)

2. Trim seams to ¼" and press in one direction.

3. Cut the first strip off approximately in the middle and discard the selvage off the 40th strip. Bring the two ends together — trim the loose end.

4. Here's the hardest part of the whole quilt top: bring the two ends together — right sides together with **no twists** in the strips. Sew together the long sides together. Your strip will now be **2 strips wide and about 800" long**.

5. Clip the folded end so that you can open the strips and press the seam in one direction.

6. Again, bring the short ends together; sew the long sides right sides together; clip the fold and press. Notice that each fold cuts the strip length in half and the seam becomes much easier to sew.

7. Repeat step 6 until you have **32 strips in width**.

8. Your top is ready to be squared up.

9. Cut **six 2½" strips** from the inner border fabric. Piece strips together end to end. Find an average. Cut two strips this length and add to quilt top. Measure opposite sides of quilt top. Cut two strips and add to quilt for top and bottom. Press seams toward outer border.

10. Cut a **5⅜" border** lengthwise from outer border fabric. Measure quilt top lengthwise for average. Cut two strips this length and add. Cut two strips for top and bottom. This will give you a finished size of approximately **61" x 77"**.

11. Layer backing, batting, and top. Quilt as desired.

12. **Bind** using 2½" strips of outer border fabric.

---

*Notes: Cut the best half — it's messy!*
